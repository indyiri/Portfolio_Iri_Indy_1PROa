Work experience

  • Waiter / Bartender

    TVL Café Genk

    2013 - 2015

    My first student job was working as a bartender and waiter at a brasserie in Genk. During this time, I learned to take orders and handle payments. In addition, I was responsible for serving orders, setting tables, restocking refrigerators, counting the cash register, and more.

    After a year, I was entrusted to manage the dining room area on my own. This became my first real test of responsibility and professionalism, especially as TV Limburg, a local television channel, regularly filmed on-site.

    Unfortunately, TVL Café Genk went bankrupt in 2015, but by then I had already secured a second job in the catering industry, namely with the catering business De Malle Koks.

  • Mortgage Application Controller

    KBC Headquarters Leuven

    2015

    The summer after I graduated from secondary school, I worked at KBC Leuven for a month. In this role, I was responsible for processing mortgage files as a second-line controller.

    The job carried significant responsibility, with a target of 10 files to be completed per day. Additionally, I had access to private accounts at KBC to verify mortgage applications, which required me to adhere strictly to client privacy and confidentiality.

    This position marked my first real work experience and gave me valuable insight into the operations of corporate banking.

  • Waiter / Bartender

    De Malle Koks

    2014 - 2017

    At the catering company "De Malle Koks", I was responsible for taking and delivering orders, but this time not in a brasserie - rather, at various other locations. We were hired for receptions, weddings, baby showers, birthday parties, and more. As part of the job, I also had to help set up and break down the events afterward.

    I gained a lot of experience in event management and learned the importance of working long hours, as the setup, event and breakdown could easily last 12 hours or more.

    After a year with the organization, I took on more responsibility and was given the opportunity to help organize events. This involved ordering large amounts of various drinks for the guests and the workers, ingredients for the chefs, and ensuring there were enough glasses, chairs, tables, fridges, etc.

    This experience proved invaluable when I later took on the role of event organizer and president of my student club, Moeder Mijnlamp.

  • Event Organizer & President

    Student organization Moeder Mijnlamp

    2017 - 2020

    In 2017, I became president of my student club in Leuven, marking my first real leadership experience. In this role, I was responsible for organizing a variety of events, including traditional singing evening ( known as "cantussen" ), trips, parties and larger occasions like Gencker Gala, annual gala ball held in Genk.

    This position carried significant responsibilities, as I was ultimately accountable for all aspects of the events - from ensuring they were profitable to guaranteeing they were enjoyable for attendees.

    After completing my one-year term as president, I remained an active member of the club's board until 2020, continuing to contribute to event planning and organization.

  • Private Tutor in High School
    Mathematics and Physics

    Freelancer

    2021 - 2024

    While serving as president and event organizer for Moeder Mijnlamp, I pursued academic bachelor's degrees in Chemistry and Industrial Engineering. Although I did not complete these programs, they allowed me to discover my deep passion for mathematics and physics.

    I successfully completed all mathematics and physics courses in both programs, which inspired me to begin tutoring secondary school students (grades 3 - 6) as a side job.

    In this role, I helped students with exercises and provided clear explanations of theoretical concepts.

  • Chemical Operator

    Nitto Belgium Nv

    2023 - 2024

    In 2023, I began working as a chemical operator at Nitto Belgium Nv in Genk. This was my first experience with full-time work. I worked in a 3-shift system in the adhesive department, where we produced various adhesive products, such as glue, tape and tires.

    This role involved a range of tasks, including safely storing raw materials, handling hazardous chemicals, controlling large-scale chemical reactions, and, most importantly, adhering to safety protocols - wearing the necessary personal protective equipment and understanding the hazardous substances we worked with.

    The job came with significant responsibility, as each operator was required to provide the right adhesive mixture to multiple production lines. Additionally, we needed to understand chemical reactions and, above all, remain calm and know how to respond if a chemical reaction went out of control.

    During my 1.5 years at Nitto, I frequently collaborated with IT professionals who were responsible for programming the various reactors and mixers. This experience sparked my interest in programming, which ultimately led me to pursue a degree in Programming at PXL.

  • Building Manager

    Apartment building 'De Melberg'

    2024 - ongoing

    In mid-2024, I took on the role of building manager at the apartment complex where I live. This role involves a variety of tasks, ranging from routine maintenance and cleaning, to emergency repairs.

    I am also responsible for making sure the apartment complex has all the necessary permits and complies with government regulations, meaning a have to organize routine inspections.

    Additionally, I am responsible for ensuring that all bills are paid on time and that each resident pays their share promptly.
